About the Stories

While essays, lectures, or even arguments about sensitive topics tend to push us ever deeper into our existing points of view, I've always felt stories have the power to do the opposite. Once we are drawn into a set of characters and their challenges we begin to have real empathy for them, reaching a place of softness from which it becomes possible to learn about an alternative view, shift our own perspectives, or even change our minds. With these short works of fiction I'm hoping to bring my readers new perspectives on gender, sexuality, relationships, and social justice.

About the Author

Sumu Tasib is a writer, maker, and scientist living in Seattle, Washington. A Boy Named Su is his first book, a collection of stories also appearing on Medium under a publication of the same name. He is currently working on a set of short stories, the first of which, "Through the Walls," has been selected for inclusion in the 2022 Seattle Erotic Arts Festival (SEAF). Su can be found on Medium or Twitter as @sumutasib and can be reached by email at sumu.tasib@gmail.com.
